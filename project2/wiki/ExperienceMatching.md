# ExperienceMatching

Experience matching is the core mechanism by which the platform connects individuals based on shared lived experiences, facilitating empathetic connections and peer support. Unlike traditional social platforms that rely on explicit categories or broad interest groups, this system aims for nuanced, semantically driven matching that acknowledges the complexity and emotional texture of human experiences.

## Key Concepts

*   **Shared Lived Experience**: The fundamental premise that empathy and effective support are most effectively transmitted between individuals who have navigated similar life challenges or circumstances.
*   **Hybrid Experience Representation**: A dual approach to encoding user experiences, combining structured, machine-filterable data (e.g., categories, timelines) with dense, semantic embedding vectors derived from narrative descriptions.
*   **Two-Stage Matching**: A process that first uses fast, approximate retrieval (embedding similarity) to identify a broad pool of candidates, followed by a more precise, LLM-powered re-ranking and filtering stage.
*   **Match Explanation**: A human-readable justification generated by an LLM for why a specific mentor-mentee pairing is suggested, crucial for building user trust and increasing acceptance rates.
*   **Affective Trajectory**: The longitudinal tracking of a user's emotional state, which can serve as an implicit signal for the quality and effectiveness of a mentor-mentee match over time.
*   **Mentor Capacity**: A critical constraint on the number of active mentees a mentor can support, designed to prevent burnout and maintain the quality of peer support.

## How It Works

The [[ExperienceStorageRetrieval]] system is central to effective matching. Each user's lived experience is captured through a hybrid approach:
1.  **Structured Record**: A JSON schema captures quantifiable aspects such as `category` (e.g., "health," "grief"), `time_since_onset_months`, `duration_months`, `current_status`, and a crucial `emotional_texture` field (e.g., ["fear", "isolation", "relief"]). This allows for precise filtering and hard constraints.
2.  **Embedding Vector**: Users provide a free-text or voice-driven narrative of their experience. This narrative is transcribed (if voice) and then embedded into a high-dimensional vector space using an LLM. This vector captures the semantic and emotional nuance that structured tags alone cannot.
