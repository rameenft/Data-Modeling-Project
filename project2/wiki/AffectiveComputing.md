# AffectiveComputing

Affective computing is the field concerned with systems that recognize, interpret, process, and simulate human affect (emotions). For an AI-powered social platform focused on shared human experiences, the ability to understand and respond to users' emotional states is fundamental to building trust and delivering functionally empathetic interactions. While AI cannot experience true empathy, it can produce responses that are *perceived* as empathetic by humans, a concept known as functional empathy.

## Key Concepts

*   **Affective Computing:** Systems capable of recognizing, interpreting, processing, and simulating human emotions.
*   **Functional Empathy:** AI-generated responses that are perceived as empathetic by human users, achieved through accurate emotion recognition and appropriate, non-judgmental acknowledgment.
*   **Distress Score:** A real-time metric (0-1) indicating a user's level of distress, crucial for triggering [[SafetyAndEscalation]] protocols.
*   **Affect Trajectory:** The tracking of a user's emotional state over multiple sessions, providing insights into their progress and the effectiveness of a [[MentorMenteeSystem]] match.
*   **Multimodal Recognition:** Combining analysis from both text and audio modalities to achieve more accurate and nuanced emotion detection.

## How It Works

Affective computing on the platform primarily focuses on recognizing emotional states from user input and using these insights to calibrate AI responses and inform platform functions.

**Emotion Recognition:**
The system employs multimodal recognition, analyzing both text and audio inputs for emotional signals.
*   **Text Analysis:** Utilizes models like GoEmotions, EmoRoBERTa, or VADER to classify sentiment (positive/negative/neutral), specific emotions (e.g., joy, sadness, anger), and distress signals (crisis language).
*   **Voice/Audio Analysis:** Extracts prosodic features (pitch, speech rate, pausing), volume dynamics, and voice quality (e.g., trembling for anxiety). This is critical as audio can reveal emotions that text alone might miss, such as sarcasm or masked distress. The [[VoiceInterface]] component provides the raw audio stream.

A crucial design decision is the handling of disfluencies in speech. While [[DialogueSystem]] components often require cleaned transcripts for NLP tasks, the raw transcript, including filler words ("um," "uh"), false starts, repetitions, and emotional breaks (crying, laughter, long silences), is vital for accurate affect analysis. These disfluencies are powerful signals of cognitive load, uncertainty, or emotional difficulty. Therefore, the [[VoiceInterface]] produces dual outputs: a raw transcript for affect analysis and a cleaned one for other NLP tasks.

**Affect Modeling and Application:**
Recognized emotional states are then modeled and applied across the platform:
*   **Distress Detection:** A real-time distress score (0-1) is generated for each utterance. A conservative threshold is applied, prioritizing false positives over false negatives to ensure timely intervention via the [[SafetyAndEscalation]] system.
*   **Engagement Quality:** Affective signals from both parties can indicate the health of a conversation. If engagement or positive affect declines, it may signal a need for a check-in or re-evaluation of the [[MentorMenteeSystem]] match.
*   **Affect Trajectory:** Beyond point-in-time scores, the system tracks emotional states across multiple sessions. A mentee trending from "anxious-high" to "hopeful-medium" over several interactions with a mentor is a strong indicator of a successful match and positive progress. This trajectory data can also feed into the [[ExperienceStorageRetrieval]] system, updating the `emotional_texture` field of a user's experience and providing a natural quality signal for mentors.
*   **Conversation Calibration:** Affective insights directly inform the [[DialogueSystem]]'s behavior and [[EmpathyDesign]]. If a user is distressed, the AI's voice (via [[VoiceInterface]]'s TTS) should be slower, softer, and lower-pitched, achieved through SSML (Speech Synthesis Markup Language) generated by the LLM. The dialogue manager enforces policies like "validation-first" and appropriate pacing, ensuring responses are emotionally attuned and avoid premature advice-giving.

## Design Implications

*   **Consent and Privacy:** Emotional state data is highly sensitive. Users must provide explicit consent for any audio analysis beyond transcription, and raw audio should never be stored beyond the session. Access controls for emotional data must be extremely tight.
*   **Parallel Processing:** The affect-service must run in parallel to the [[DialogueSystem]] and [[VoiceInterface]] components within the [[PlatformArchitecture]]. Affective scoring should not block dialogue generation to maintain low latency.
*   **Dynamic Empathy:** Affective computing enables dynamic adjustment of AI responses and voice prosody. The LLM can generate SSML alongside text, allowing the AI's voice to reflect appropriate warmth, seriousness, or softness based on the user's detected emotional state, significantly enhancing [[EmpathyDesign]].
*   **Implicit Quality Metric:** The affect trajectory provides a powerful, objective signal for evaluating [[MentorMenteeSystem]] match quality and mentee progress, complementing or even reducing reliance on explicit user ratings.
