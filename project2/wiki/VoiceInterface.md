# VoiceInterface

## VoiceInterface

The Voice Interface is a foundational component of an AI-powered social platform for shared human experiences, enabling natural, emotionally expressive interaction through Speech-to-Text (STT) and Text-to-Speech (TTS) technologies. Voice is considered the optimal modality for this platform, as it allows users to articulate complex emotional narratives more naturally and with less cognitive load than typing, especially when navigating difficult experiences like grief or illness.

### Key Concepts

*   **Speech-to-Text (STT):** The process of converting spoken language into written text. Essential for transcribing user narratives and dialogue.
*   **Text-to-Speech (TTS):** The process of converting written text into spoken language. Crucial for the AI's empathetic responses.
*   **Streaming Architecture:** A system design where data (audio, text) is processed and transmitted in continuous chunks rather than waiting for full completion, vital for low-latency, real-time conversation.
*   **Latency Budget:** The maximum acceptable delay between a user's action (e.g., speaking) and the system's response, critical for natural conversational flow.
*   **Disfluency Handling:** The processing of non-lexical elements in speech (filler words, false starts, pauses), which can be either removed for clarity or retained as signals for [[AffectiveComputing]].
*   **SSML (Speech Synthesis Markup Language):** An XML-based markup language used to control aspects of speech synthesis, such as prosody (pitch, rate, volume), pauses, and emphasis.

### How It Works

The Voice Interface integrates STT and TTS pipelines within a real-time, streaming [[PlatformArchitecture]] to facilitate seamless voice-driven dialogue.

**Speech-to-Text Pipeline:**
User voice input is captured and processed by STT systems. Options range from open-source models like OpenAI's Whisper (known for multilingual capabilities and robustness to accents/noise) to cloud-based services like Google Speech-to-Text, AssemblyAI, or Deepgram Nova-2 (offering low latency, speaker diarization, and specialized models). For sensitive contexts, on-device Whisper (e.g., tiny/base models) is prioritized to ensure maximum privacy, preventing raw audio from leaving the user's device.

A critical aspect of STT for this platform is **disfluency handling**. While most downstream Natural Language Processing (NLP) tasks benefit from cleaned transcripts (removing filler words, false starts), these disfluencies are vital signals for [[AffectiveComputing]]. For example, a high rate of filler words can indicate cognitive load or uncertainty, while long silences might signal grief or trauma processing. Therefore, the system generates a dual output: a raw transcript for affect analysis and a cleaned transcript for core NLP tasks like [[ExperienceMatching]] and [[DialogueSystem]] context.

**Text-to-Speech Pipeline:**
The AI's responses, generated by the [[DialogueSystem]], are converted into natural-sounding speech using advanced neural TTS models. Providers like OpenAI TTS, ElevenLabs, Google Cloud TTS, and Azure TTS offer near-human naturalness, with some providing expressive styles (e.g., "empathetic," "sad") and voice cloning capabilities.

**SSML** is extensively used to control prosody, ensuring the AI's voice conveys appropriate warmth, seriousness, or empathy. For instance, a slower rate, lower pitch, and softer volume can indicate attentiveness during sensitive moments. The [[DialogueSystem]]'s LLM is designed to generate SSML markup alongside the response text, allowing dynamic calibration of the AI's voice based on the user's emotional state and the conversation phase. This prevents robotic or tone-deaf responses, which can actively damage trust, as highlighted in [[EmpathyDesign]] principles.

**Streaming Architecture and Latency Design:**
To achieve a natural conversational feel, the entire voice interface operates on a streaming architecture. Audio is sent from the client in small chunks via websockets. STT processes these chunks, returning partial transcripts rapidly, with the final transcript generated upon end-of-utterance detection (VAD). In parallel, the [[AffectiveComputing]] service analyzes the audio and text for emotional signals. The [[DialogueSystem]] then processes the transcript, and its LLM-generated response is streamed to the TTS service, which begins synthesizing and streaming audio back to the client as soon as the first sentence is available.

The target latency budget is aggressive: aiming for the user to hear the AI's response within ~1.4 seconds of finishing their speech. This requires sub-second performance at each stage: VAD end-of-speech (~200ms), STT transcription (~300ms), LLM first token (~500ms), and TTS first chunk (~300ms). This streaming approach, combined with the ability for users to "barge-in" and interrupt the AI mid-speech, is non-negotiable for a truly responsive and empathetic conversational experience.

**Voice Persona Design:**
The choice of AI voice is a crucial [[EmpathyDesign]] consideration. The persona should be consistent, not too young or authoritative, and avoid being overly cheerful in sensitive contexts. It should also ideally be non-gendered to maximize relatability and avoid bias. Users form a relationship with the voice, making its design integral to the platform's perceived empathy.

### Design Implications

*   **Prioritize Streaming:** Implement streaming at every stage (audio capture, STT, LLM response generation, TTS) to meet the strict latency budget required for natural, real-time conversation.
*   **Dual Transcript Output:** Design the STT pipeline to produce both raw (for [[AffectiveComputing]]) and cleaned (for [[DialogueSystem]] NLP) transcripts to leverage disfluencies as emotional signals.
*   **Dynamic SSML Generation:** Integrate SSML generation directly into the [[DialogueSystem]]'s LLM prompt to allow for contextually appropriate and empathetic prosody in TTS responses, critical for [[EmpathyDesign]].
